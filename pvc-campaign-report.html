05 · Key Takeaways

Wins, Watch Points & Mistakes

A direct summary of what the data shows — no filler, no opinion beyond what the numbers indicate.

✓ Win

CTR is Strong at 5.61%

The industry benchmark for Facebook ads is roughly 0.9%–1.5%. A 5.61% CTR indicates the ad creative and copy are resonating enough to get people to click. This is a meaningful early signal.

✓ Win

Cost Per Click is Very Low

At $0.20 per link click, the campaign is acquiring traffic at a very efficient rate. The average CPC for service-based businesses on Facebook typically ranges from $1.00–$3.00+.

✓ Win

Instagram Outperformed Facebook

Instagram delivered higher reach (~360) and more results than Facebook (~220 reach). This tells us the audience on Instagram is more responsive to this specific ad creative.

✓ Win

Ages 55–64 Are the Top Responders

With 9 total results, the 55–64 age group outperformed all others. This age group also shows the most balanced gender response, suggesting a broader appeal within that bracket.

⚠ Watch Point

Hook Rate at 36% — Room to Improve

36% of viewers watched past the opening seconds. A good benchmark for hook rate is 40–60%. The current number means 64% of viewers decided to scroll away within the very first moments of the ad.

⚠ Watch Point

Campaign Is Still in Learning Phase

Facebook's algorithm needs approximately 50 optimization events to exit the learning phase. With 31 clicks over 3 days, the campaign has not yet reached that threshold. Current data is directional only.

⚠ Watch Point

18–24 Clicks Aren't Matching Tax Intent

5 results came from 18–24 year old men, which may not be the highest-intent audience for tax consultations. Whether those clicks converted to bookings is the key question.

✗ Mistake

Video Retention Drops Off Too Quickly

The average play time is 3 seconds on a 1:07 video. That is 4.4% completion. Most viewers are not receiving the message, the offer, or the call to action. The video's opening is not holding attention long enough.

✗ Mistake

Traffic Objective May Not Match the Goal

The campaign is optimized for Link Clicks (Traffic), not for Leads or Conversions. If the goal is booked consultations, optimizing for traffic only tells Facebook to find people who click — not people who book. The two audiences can be very different.

✗ Mistake

No Retargeting Layer Visible

Based on the data shown, there is one ad running with no indication of a retargeting campaign for people who watched the video or visited the landing page. People who clicked but didn't book are not being followed up with.

06 · Next Steps

Actionable Suggestions

Based on the data above, here are specific adjustments to consider for the next phase of the campaign.

1
Video

Rework the first 5 seconds of the video

The biggest drop in retention happens between 0:00 and 0:05. Consider opening with a direct, pattern-interrupting statement or a question that immediately speaks to the viewer's pain point — for example, "Still paying more in taxes than you should?" or a bold on-screen text overlay in the first frame. The goal is to stop the scroll before the viewer decides to move on.

2
Campaign Objective

Consider switching the objective to Leads or Conversions

If the end goal is booked consultations, a Leads campaign with a native Facebook Lead Form removes the step of visiting an external page. Alternatively, a Conversions campaign with a Meta Pixel installed on the booking confirmation page will teach the algorithm to find people more likely to actually book — not just click.

3
Audience

Build a narrowed audience around the 55–64 age group

The 55–64 bracket is your top-performing segment with 9 results and the most balanced gender split. Consider creating a separate ad set targeting 45–65+ specifically, with messaging that speaks to retirement planning, late-career tax strategy, or estate considerations — which are more relevant financial concerns for that demographic.

4
Retargeting

Set up a video view retargeting audience

Anyone who watched 25–50% or more of the video has shown meaningful intent. Create a Custom Audience based on video views, then run a separate retargeting ad to that group — ideally with a different creative (a testimonial, a specific offer, or a direct "Book Now" message). This audience is warm and significantly cheaper to convert.

5
Audience

Review whether 18–24 is the right audience to include

5 clicks came from 18–24 year old men. Unless PVC Tax Group specifically serves younger clients, this segment may be consuming budget without converting to consultations. After more data accumulates, consider excluding 13–24 from the targeting if conversion data confirms low intent from that group.

6
Budget & Patience

Allow the campaign to run through the full learning phase

Avoid making significant changes (budget adjustments over 20%, audience changes, creative swaps) until the campaign has gathered at least 50 optimization events or 7–10 days of data. Frequent changes reset the learning phase and cost more in the long run. Small, patient adjustments are more effective at this stage.

7
Creative

Test a static image or carousel alongside the video

Not all audiences engage with video. Running an A/B test with a clean static image ad (featuring a strong headline, social proof, and clear CTA) alongside the video will reveal which format your audience responds to better. Instagram in particular performs well with high-contrast, text-forward static creatives.
